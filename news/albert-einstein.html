Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was born in Germany and he was really smart. He discovered that time may not be the same on all planets; this is called the Theory of Relativity. He discussed how a year in a galaxy far away might be the same as 7 years on Earth.

He was also known as a very funny man, always making smart jokes and pulling pranks on people. Once, when he was on tour discussing his research, his driver turned to him and said:
"Mr. Einstein, I've been listening to you give this talk 100 times already. I'm sure I could do it just as good as you."
Einstein took him seriously and the two men changed clothes, with Albert posing as his driver and vice-versa. (Apparently, the driver also had a big moustache).
The disguised driver arrives at the conference and receives a very warm welcome from everybody. He then begins talking about the Theory of Relativity and the real Einstein, sitting in the audience, is impressed by how much he knows.
When the Einstein on stage finishes, a man from the public rises and starts asking a lot of questions, because he believes the theory too complicated, too hard to understand and generally incorrect. To which the disguised driver replies:
"Sir, it is a very easy thing to understand. So easy, that even my driver can explain it."
He then invited the real Einstein to the stage, who gave all the explanations. When the two men left, nobody had figured out the switch.
